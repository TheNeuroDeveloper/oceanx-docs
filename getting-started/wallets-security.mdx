# Wallets & Security

OceanX is designed around self-custody.

Your OceanX wallets are non-custodial, meaning OceanX cannot access, freeze or transfer your assets.

## Wallet structure

When you create an account, OceanX initially creates:

- One Solana wallet
- One EVM-compatible wallet

The EVM wallet can be used with major EVM-compatible chains supported by OceanX.

## MPC wallet infrastructure

OceanX uses an MPC wallet structure through embedded wallet infrastructure.

MPC, or Multi-Party Computation, avoids relying on a single exposed private key. Instead, signing is handled through secure key shares.

Benefits include:

- Reduced single point of failure
- Improved recovery options
- Smooth embedded wallet experience
- Secure on-device signing
- No repeated wallet popups for every action

## Privy-powered embedded wallets

OceanX uses Privy-powered embedded wallets.

This allows users to:

- Sign in with email
- Use passkeys
- Access wallets without browser extensions
- Maintain control over their wallet
- Export private keys where supported

## Passkeys

Passkeys are recommended for account security.

They provide a modern login experience using secure device-based authentication such as Face ID, Touch ID, Windows Hello or hardware-backed credentials.

## Multi-factor authentication

Users should enable additional security wherever available.

Recommended settings include:

- Passkeys
- MFA
- Recovery methods
- Secure email access
- Trusted devices

## Wallet control

OceanX does not custody user assets.

Users remain in control of their wallets. OceanX cannot withdraw funds on a user's behalf.

<Warning>
Never share passwords, private keys, recovery credentials or device access with anyone claiming to represent OceanX.
</Warning>

## FAQ

### Can OceanX move my funds?

No. OceanX wallets are non-custodial.

### Do I need a seed phrase?

OceanX is designed to avoid the traditional seed phrase onboarding experience. Wallet export and recovery options may be available where supported.

### Should I use passkeys?

Yes. Passkeys are strongly recommended.
