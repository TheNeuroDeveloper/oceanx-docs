# Scout

Scout is OceanX's real time discovery terminal.

It helps traders monitor launches, migrations, trends and market activity without switching between tools.

<Note>
Scout is planned for release in July 2026.
</Note>

## Scout views

Scout includes several discovery views.

### Latest

Newly launched assets.

### Graduating

Assets approaching migration from a launchpad bonding curve to a DEX liquidity pool.

### Graduated

Assets that have completed migration.

### Trending

Assets gaining market attention.

### Top

Strong performing assets.

### Boosted

Boosted or promoted assets.

### Metas

Narrative based categories such as AI, gaming, memes or DeFi.

## Token cards

Scout token cards may display:

- Price
- Market cap
- Liquidity
- Volume
- Holder count
- Buy and sell activity
- Migration status
- Sniper percentage (automated bot buys at launch)
- Insider percentage (developer or team linked wallets)
- Developer holdings
- Top holder concentration
- Risk indicators

## Filters

Users can modify Scout using custom and preset filters.

Examples include:

- Market cap
- Liquidity
- Age
- Volume
- Holder count
- Sniper percentage
- Insider percentage
- Developer percentage
- Top 10 holder percentage
- Launch platform
- Migration state

Scout columns cannot currently be dragged or reordered, but users can modify results through a wide range of filters.

## Risk labels

Scout may show simple risk labels:

- Low Risk
- Medium Risk
- High Risk

These labels are designed to help users quickly interpret token risk, but they should not replace personal research.

## Quick Buy presets

Users can configure their own Quick Buy presets.

OceanX provides three preset slots directly on Scout, and users can adjust them based on their own trading preferences.

## Watchlists

Users can create custom watchlists to monitor assets over time.

## Alerts

Scout initially supports sound alerts.

Future alert channels may include:

- Push notifications
- Telegram
- Discord
- Email

## FAQ

### Can I customize my presets?

Yes. Users set their own Quick Buy presets across three configurable slots.

### Can I create watchlists?

Yes. Watchlists are supported.

### Are risk labels financial advice?

No. Risk labels are informational only.

### How many Quick Buy preset slots are there?

Three.
